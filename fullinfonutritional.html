Sweet Potato and Chickpea Curry

Category: Dinner

Serving Size: 1 cup (approximately 240g)

Prep Time: 10 minutes (preparation) + 30 minutes (cooking)

Calories: 350 per serving

Ingredients

Vegetables & Legumes

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, grated

Spices & Seasonings

  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional for heat)

Optional Serving Suggestions

  • Cooked rice (white, brown, or basmati)
  • Naan bread
  • Quinoa (for a higher protein option)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Yogurt or coconut yogurt, for a creamy topping

Health Benefits

Rich in Dietary Fiber

Sweet potatoes and chickpeas are both high in dietary fiber, which aids digestion, helps maintain healthy blood sugar levels, and promotes a feeling of fullness, which can support weight management.

High in Antioxidants

Sweet potatoes are an excellent source of beta-carotene (Vitamin A), a powerful antioxidant that supports eye health, boosts the immune system, and protects against oxidative stress.

Plant-Based Protein

Chickpeas provide a good amount of plant-based protein, essential for muscle repair and growth. This makes the meal particularly beneficial for vegetarians and vegans who may need alternative protein sources.

Anti-Inflammatory Properties

The spices used in the curry, such as turmeric, cumin, and ginger, have anti-inflammatory properties. Turmeric, in particular, contains curcumin, which has been shown to reduce inflammation in the body.
